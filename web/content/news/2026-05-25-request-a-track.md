The station isn't a jukebox you boss around, but it does take requests. You ask in plain words, and the DJ decides how to honour it from the music that's actually in the library.

## How to ask

Open the request panel in the player and type what you're after. You don't need an exact track. Any of these work:

- "Something like Khruangbin"
- "Pick up the energy a bit"
- "More of this, but older"
- A specific song or artist by name

Send it. The DJ reads what you meant (the artist, the mood, the intent), finds candidates in your library, and lines one up.

## What happens next

Your pick joins the queue and plays for everyone, because there's only one stream. When it comes on, the DJ usually says a quick line introducing it, sometimes nodding to the request itself. It won't cut off the current song, because tracks always finish, so think of it as leaning over and asking the DJ, not grabbing the aux cord.

## Why it's good

It makes the station feel live. A request changes what the whole room hears next, and the DJ reacts to it on air instead of silently swapping a track. That little back-and-forth is the difference between listening to a feed and being somewhere.
