Most music apps give everyone their own private queue. SUB/WAVE does the opposite. It runs one live stream, and everyone listening hears the same thing at the same time. There's a DJ between the tracks. The DJ is an AI, and the music is your own library.

## The idea

It's a radio station for one person to run and anyone to tune into. You point it at your music, give the DJ a personality, and it goes on air. From then on it picks tracks, reads the odd line between songs, checks the weather, marks the hour. You don't build a playlist. You start a station and let it run.

## How it works

Four small pieces do the work. Navidrome holds your music. An LLM is the DJ's brain and decides what plays next and what to say. A text-to-speech voice turns those lines into audio. Liquidsoap and Icecast mix it all into one stream and broadcast it. That's the whole machine.

The DJ pulls from what you actually own, so the station sounds like your taste, not an algorithm's idea of it.

## Tune in

Open the player and hit play. If you're setting up your own, the [setup guide](/setup) gets you on air in about ten minutes. Everything past the first three settings is configured from the admin screen: the DJ's voice, its personality, how chatty it is, the look of the player.

It's your station. It just happens to run itself.
